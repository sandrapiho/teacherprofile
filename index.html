Hi!

I am Sandra, a teacher of English as a foreign language (EFL) working in Gustav Adolf Grammar School and at the same time working towards obtaining my teaching qualifications in the University of Tartu.

Before going into Education, I studied Comparative Literature in the United Kingdom. I received my undergraduate diploma from the University of Kent in England and later did my Master's in the University of Edinburgh in Scotland and graduated in 2025.

My previous experience in teaching includes working as a substitute teacher during Covid pandemic in 2020 when I was hired by a local school in Estonia. I got the chance to work with students of different levels from primary school up to upper secondary years. After My studies in the United Kingdom, I returned to Estonia and started working in Tallinn as a teacher of English.

As an EFL teacher my aim is to create a learning environment for students that helps them feel confident, supported and motivated in the process of learning. I strive to create a classroom environment where students feel safe to take risks, make mistakes, ask questions, and grow at their own pace because I believe that a safe learning space helps students thrive.

In order to achieve that I aim to create lessons that are well balanced, incorporating both structured learning and challenging tasks as well as creativity and fun. In doing so I hope to nurture students’ curiosity, resilience, and their sense of belonging.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy centers around the idea that language is best learned through its use. The main reason for studying a language is communication. So, in order to achieve that end goal, using language in its learning process is imperative.


In addition, I believe that learners should feel safe in their learning environment. They should be allowed to make mistakes and not feel threatened by it. Making mistakes is an integral part of any learning process and should not make a student feel like they are unsafe.

Finally, In my opinion, learning should be fun! There is nothing more detrimental to the motivation of students than boredom.


Therefore, I design lessons that promote authentic communication and provide opportunities for learners to express themselves. I also strive to create a safe environment for the students and enjoyable activities to make learning fun.


Teaching Experience

During my teaching experience I have developed a clearer understanding of my strengths and weaknesses, as well as my identity as a teacher.


My current strengths lie in lesson planning, creating a safe and supportive learning environment, and developing activities that help practice communication. My lessong are planned logically and I am confident in selecting, adapting, and creating materials which support the lessons. I provide a safe environment for my students which helps learners feel comfortable taking risks in their learning process. I use pair work, group work, and class discussion in order to promote communication and collaboration. I also provide visual supports (like presentations), sentence starters, example texts, and guided practice to help learners improve their skills in class. I differentiate tasks by complexity, support, or outcome, ensuring that all learners can participate meaningfully.


However, I also have areas in which I hope to continue to develop my skills. One such area is balancing fluency focused (communication) and accuracy focused (grammar) work. While I am confident in designing both tasks, I find it difficult to integrate the two in a cohesive manner that does not interrupt the flow of the lesson. Another area I struggle in is assessment and feedback. Although I use formative assessment effectively, I wish to actively involve learners in the process using self assessment and goal setting. My goal is to work together with my students, get them to take active part of the assessment and feedback process, and , help them develop autonomy regarding their learning process.
