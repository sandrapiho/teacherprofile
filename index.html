Hi!

I am Sandra, a teacher of English as a foreign language (EFL) working in Gustav Adolf Grammar School and at the same time working towards obtaining my teaching qualifications in the University of Tartu.

Before going into Education, I studied Comparative Literature in the United Kingdom. I received my undergraduate diploma from the University of Kent in England and later did my Master's in the University of Edinburgh in Scotland and graduated in 2025.

My previous experience in teaching includes working as a substitute teacher during Covid pandemic in 2020 when I was hired by a local school in Estonia. I got the chance to work with students of different levels from primary school up to upper secondary years. After My studies in the United Kingdom, I returned to Estonia and started working in Tallinn as a teacher of English.

As an EFL teacher my aim is to create a learning environment for students that helps them feel confident, supported and motivated in the process of learning. I strive to create a classroom environment where students feel safe to take risks, make mistakes, ask questions, and grow at their own pace because I believe that a safe learning space helps students thrive.

In order to achieve that I aim to create lessons that are well balanced, incorporating both structured learning and challenging tasks as well as creativity and fun. In doing so I hope to nurture students’ curiosity, resilience, and their sense of belonging.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy centers around the idea that language is best learned through its use. The main reason for studying a language is communication. So, in order to achieve that end goal, using language in its learning process is imperative.


In addition, I believe that learners should feel safe in their learning environment. They should be allowed to make mistakes and not feel threatened by it. Making mistakes is an integral part of any learning process and should not make a student feel like they are unsafe.

Finally, In my opinion, learning should be fun! There is nothing more detrimental to the motivation of students than boredom.


Therefore, I design lessons that promote authentic communication and provide opportunities for learners to express themselves. I also strive to create a safe environment for the students and enjoyable activities to make learning fun.


Teaching Experience

My teaching experience so far has allowed me to develop a clearer understanding of my strengths, weaknesses, and identity as an EFL teacher. Reflecting on my completed EPOSTL descriptors, I recognise that my strongest areas lie in lesson planning, creating a supportive learning environment, and developing communicative activities.


I consistently plan lessons with clear linguistic and communicative objectives, ensuring that tasks build logically toward meaningful use of English. I am confident in selecting, adapting, and creating materials, especially authentic texts, to suit learners’ proficiency levels and interests. These strengths align with the EPOSTL descriptors related to planning, selecting materials, and supporting learner engagement.


Another key strength is my ability to establish a positive classroom climate. I prioritise rapport, encouragement, and emotional safety, which helps learners feel comfortable taking risks in English. I use a variety of interaction patterns — pair work, group work, and whole class discussion—to promote communication and collaboration. According to the EPOSTL descriptors on classroom management and interaction, I demonstrate competence in facilitating learner to learner communication and responding flexibly to classroom dynamics.


I regularly provide visual supports, sentence starters, model texts, and guided practice to help learners access new language. I differentiate tasks by complexity, support, or outcome, ensuring that all learners can participate meaningfully. These practices correspond to EPOSTL descriptors related to supporting individual learning needs and adapting instruction.


However, my self assessment also highlights areas where I continue to develop. One of my main challenges is balancing fluency focused and accuracy focused work. While I am confident in designing communicative tasks, I sometimes find it difficult to integrate systematic grammar practice without interrupting the flow of communication. I aim to strengthen my ability to provide timely, focused feedback on form while maintaining learner engagement. This relates to EPOSTL descriptors on error correction, feedback, and integrating language systems.


Another area for growth is assessment literacy. Although I use formative assessment effectively, I want to deepen my skills in designing valid, reliable assessment tasks that measure communicative competence. I also aim to involve learners more actively in self assessment and goal setting, helping them develop greater autonomy. These goals align with EPOSTL descriptors on assessment, feedback, and learner reflection.


Throughout my teaching experience, I have made significant progress in professional reflection and collaboration. I regularly analyse my lessons, identify areas for improvement, and experiment with new strategies. I seek feedback from colleagues and engage with research on EFL pedagogy. This reflective approach has helped me refine my practice and align it more closely with communicative and learner centred principles.


My accomplishments include successfully implementing task based learning sequences, integrating multimodal resources, and supporting learners with diverse linguistic backgrounds. I have also grown in my ability to adapt lessons in real time, respond to unexpected learner needs, and maintain a positive, inclusive classroom environment. Overall, my teaching experience has strengthened my identity as a reflective, learner centred EFL teacher.


The EPOSTL descriptors have helped me articulate my strengths, recognise areas for development, and set meaningful goals for continued growth. I am committed to deepening my expertise in assessment, feedback, and the integration of form focused instruction within communicative contexts. As I continue to evolve professionally, I aim to maintain a balance between structure and creativity, accuracy and fluency, and guidance and learner autonomy.
