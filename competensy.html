Competences

General Competences

Comprehension skills


1. Analysing and synthesising - using systematic reasoning in discussion, understanding processes, phenomena and explaining the relationships between them, the strengths and weaknesses of alternative choices when making decisions, and creating new units from the knowledge gained and reaching necessary conclusions.

2. Learning skills - selecting and adapting effective learning strategies to acquire new competencies, and being able to purposefully manage motivation and emotions related to management.

3. Innovation competence ¬ approaching difficulties and problems from different perspectives, seeking creative and effective solutions to them and applying novel approaches, methods and materials, being ready to cope with uncertainty and the possibility of error.


Self-management skills


4. Based on values - following the universal human and organizational values and principles of their activities.

5. Resilience - coping with changes in feelings, thoughts and behaviour, responding adequately and appropriately and restoring the balance necessary for self-care.


Social skills


6. Communication skills - creating, maintaining and developing trusting, cooperative relationships with other people, by expressing themselves clearly and politely. Noticing the needs, expectations and emotions of oneself and others and adapts communication by supporting a positive and understanding social environment.

7. Collaboration skills - setting goals together with others and working effectively to achieve them, taking into account the necessary points of view.

8. Digital competence - having a positive attitude towards the meaningful use of digital tools, environments and content. Implementing and adapting them effectively, ethically and responsibly in their work. Having acquired the necessary digital competences.


Self-care and Professionalism

In my work as a teacher, I prioritise my overall well‑being by taking care of my mental, physical, social and emotional health, knowing that a balanced teacher can better support students. I am intentional about maintaining a healthy boundary between my work and personal life, allowing me to stay focused, present and energised in the classroom. I actively shape my career path through ongoing reflection, setting goals for my development and seeking opportunities that strengthen my practice. I keep my knowledge up to date by engaging with new research, innovations in teaching and learning, and current scientific literature. I also apply a research‑informed approach to improving my professionalism, regularly analysing my teaching, testing new strategies and using evidence to guide my growth.


1. Maintaining health - taking care of one's mental, physical, social and emotional well-being.

2. Maintaining balance - taking care of one's work and private life balance.

3. Shaping a career path - consciously shaping one's career path and learning as a teacher, based on reflection on one's professional activities.

4. Updating knowledge - keeping oneself up to date with innovations in the field and teaching and learning, scientific literature and research.

5. Applying a research approach to developing one's professionalism - analysing and improving one's teaching skills by applying a research approach.


Learner Support

In my work as a teacher, I make it a priority to truly get to know each learner and group, paying attention to their backgrounds, strengths and specific characteristics. I build warm, trusting relationships and create a safe, inclusive environment where every student feels supported in their development. When planning and teaching, I follow the curriculum carefully while adapting lessons to learners’ ages, abilities, needs and overall well‑being, including integrating movement, active learning and health‑promoting routines. I select, adapt and create learning materials that suit my students, and I present content in an evidence‑based way that sparks curiosity and supports deep learning. Together with learners, I develop and uphold shared values, and I intentionally support the growth of their general competences. I guide students to take responsibility for their own learning, staying aware of how stereotypes can influence attitudes and avoiding reinforcing them. Through constructive, learner‑inclusive feedback and assessment, I help students understand their progress and take meaningful next steps in their learning.


1. Getting to know learners - getting to know each learner and group of learners, including their specific characteristics.

2. Building relationships, shaping the environment and caring for learners - building good relationships with learners, creating a safe and inclusive environment for learning and caring about the development of each learner.

3. Planning and teaching - planning teaching competently, based on the curriculum. Participating in the development of the curriculum. Treating the learning process as a whole and adapting it, based on the age, abilities, needs and mental and physical well-being of learners. Implementing health-promoting activities, including active and movement-integrated learning activities, stretching and movement breaks.

4. Selecting, adapting, compiling and sharing learning materials - selecting, adapting and compiling appropriate learning materials for learners, taking into account the specific characteristics of learners.

5. Content management - addressing content in a scientific and evidence-based manner and generating interest in the subject and supporting deep learning.

6. Value development and adherence - developing and adhereing to values agreed upon together with learners.

7. Development of general competences - supporting the development of learners' general competences.

8. Enabling learners in a targeted manner and actively guideing them to take responsibility for their own learning and development. Being aware of the impact of stereotypes, including in their own attitudes, and avoiding reinforcing them in their work.

9. Feedback and assessment - providing learners with constructive feedback on the learning process and learning outcomes and assessing learners using appropriate and learner-inclusive methods.

Contribution to the Institution and Colleagues

In my work, I guide my decisions by the goals and values of my organization, as well as by my own professional responsibilities. I collaborate actively with colleagues and often initiate joint efforts that strengthen our shared practice, while also contributing to a supportive and safe working environment. I help colleagues develop professionally by sharing methodologies, materials and practical insights from my own experience. I maintain open, respectful communication with parents, exchanging information clearly and applying sound communication principles to support each learner. I also handle information and data responsibly, following confidentiality and data‑ protection guidelines, assessing the impact of the information I use and making thoughtful decisions about how it is processed.


1. Goal-oriented and values-based - guiding by the organization's goals and values and their own rights and obligations.

2. Collaboration with colleagues in their organization - acting collaboratively and initiating collaboration.

3. Safe working environment - creating a supportive and safe working environment with colleagues.

4. Supporting the professional development of colleagues - supporting the professional development of colleagues by sharing methodologies and learning materials.

5. Communication and cooperation with parents - cooperating with parents, exchanging and mediating information and applying the basic principles of communication.

6. Information and data in the organization - processing information responsibly, following the principles of confidentiality and data protection, assessing the value and impact of information and making decisions about its processing.


Contribution to the General System of Education

I contribute actively to the wider educational community by participating in the work of professional associations and staying engaged with developments in the field. I see myself as an ambassador for the teaching profession and strive to model professionalism, care and integrity in ways that help build a positive public image of teachers. I also share pedagogical knowledge beyond my own classroom by taking part in educational events, research activities or project groups, helping to strengthen understanding of teaching and learning in society.


1. Cooperation and contribution to the work of field or professional associations - participating in the activities of field or professional associations.

2. Creating a positive image of the teaching profession - being a role model in the role of a teacher and contributing to creating a positive image of teachers in society and valuing the teaching profession.

3. Dissemination of pedagogical knowledge in society - participating in public events in the field of education, research and/or project groups.
